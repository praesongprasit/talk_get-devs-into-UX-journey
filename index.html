Dear Testers

A love letter
from your humble UI Front End Developer

Prae Songprasit | @lackofcolours

Prae, UI Front End Developer for 6 years.

Specialise in scalable CSS & Semantic HTML for large scale sites.

Like Tourism New Zealand, and Xero's customer help portal.

This means hundreds of templates, thousand components variation.

To keep things concise, I'm going to refer to Testers, QAs, Test Engineers and etc, as simply testers. And Front End Developer 'FED'.

Why?

Create empathy and show appreciation

Am getting tired of testers and developers antagonising eachother. Empathy!

Want to start, by show my appreciations, and share what I've learnt over the years

Became a better developer because of them... you!

But first, I must apologise for my past, and perhaps present shameful behavior

Sorry for

  • rolling my eyes at you
  • being a bit curt
  • taking you for granted

Roll eyes

  1. Focused on hard technical skills within my desipline.
  2. Hung out with developers. Picked up bad attitude
  3. Known issue, or common sense
  4. See you approaching my desk

Curt

  1. Deadline pressure. Last thing I want to hear is an obscure bug that requires support
  2. Another IE9 bug

Taken for granted

  1. Didn't leave enough time to test
  2. Design delay, dev prob, testers get short end of the stick
  3. Forced to cram work in, or take short cuts
  4. Pressure on you, undermine qualities
  1. Over the years, I've also learnt that QAs are like FEDs
  2. There are FEDs who are visual, and FEDs who are more like backend
  3. Come in different shapes, personality, and technical skills
  4. I have many things to thank each type of testers for

Visual testers

Thanks for

  • spotting small style changes
  • showing me the empty spaces
  • slaving through manual visual regression
  • telling me something looks incomplete

Type who focus on the visual, design, alignment and layout side of things

Invaluable when making a UI pattern library. The 20 instances of buttons!

  1. Old codebase colours into new higher contrast
  2. When there is no content, no space. Margin and padding
  3. FED landscape changes very quickly. Conventions change.
    There are more ways to make things more efficient. Hence refactor.
  4. Some projects, we FED are the designers. I need to be told!

UX Testers

Thanks for

  • asking me dumb but neccessary questions
  • showing me our error pages
  • using our product like a normal user

These people are thorough with their interaction and flow between components and pages

  1. Stop looking so guilty and lead with 'may I ask a dumb question'
    Nothing is dumb. Irrelevant at worst
  2. Error page is one thing we always forget
  3. Click around! If it's taking you where you don't expect, I have more ammunition to talk to designers

PM testers

Thanks for

  • reminding me to use analytics
  • knowing the business logic better than I do
  • managing the * out of my workflow
  • logging beautifully descriptive tickets

This breed of testers have business logic and process clear, in their head

  1. Or I'll try to cover every single edge case & waste my time on 0.5 of the users, or who I should focus my efforts on
  2. I make dumb interactive components. I don't know how many places this button needs to go, explain and can alter design
  3. Asking me hard questions, like how I work. Explain your process when I don't have a clue
  4. Clear issue outlined. Has enough experience to if this is a FED or BED bug. Screenshots & Gifs

Technical Testers

Thanks for

  • knowing how to GitHub
  • continuously improving automated test
  • replicating gloriously technical issues

Often ex-developer, or just been upskilling and learning python

  1. Review my PR, and go update automated test suite. No word needed
  2. I haven't a practical clue. Save everyone time
  3. Which browser, which OS, which viewport size, which zoom level, after how many clicks?

Platform specialist testers

Thanks for

  • sharing wealth of knowledge
  • creating test content in our 20 environments

Generally married to a platform. Custom to company, Salesforce, Wordpress, and etc

  1. Steps to display x, y. Explain how it came about. Past implementation. History
  2. UI and content goes hand in hand. Designers have imagined one. We deal with the real

Thanks for

  • Infinite patience
  • Flexibility
  • Attention to detail
  • Unofficial documentation
  • Helping me grow

Finally...

Some of the ways I code changed forever because of some very memerable bugs

Now I know:

  1. Automtatically make the whole block clickable
  2. Visited state's hover colour flickers
  3. Never assume a button fits all content
  4. To come to you when I feel lost

Help us, help you

  • Tell us what you need
  • Tell us your test process
  • No questions are dumb. Just irrelevant
  1. What kind of information/handover note is useful?
  2. When do you need to start automating those new templates?

We're a team

  1. We need you to test our pumkin pie/product
  2. Too sweet? Turn an audience off?
  3. Too hard? Not suitable for older people with bad teeth
  4. Too small a piece? No good for people with large hands?
  5. Lets make the best pie out there

Again, thanks!
